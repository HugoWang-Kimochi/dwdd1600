Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Here are some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence (1776) — the ones you'll probably hear about the most in U.S. history:

John Hancock
President of the Continental Congress. He had the first and largest signature on the Declaration of Independence. His bold signature is why Americans sometimes say “Put your John Hancock” to mean “sign your name.”
Thomas Jefferson
Primary author of the Declaration of Independence. Later became the 3rd President of the United States. He wrote the famous phrase “All men are created equal.”
Benjamin Franklin
Inventor, diplomat, and writer. He was the oldest signer at age 70. He is associated with the quote, “We must all hang together, or most assuredly we shall all hang separately.”
John Adams
Strong advocate for independence who helped convince Congress to declare independence. Later became the 2nd President of the United States.
Samuel Adams
Revolutionary political leader associated with the Boston Tea Party. He was a cousin of John Adams and a major organizer of resistance against British rule.
Richard Henry Lee
Introduced the resolution in Congress that led directly to the Declaration of Independence being drafted and adopted.

There were 56 total signers, representing the 13 colonies. Many of them risked their wealth, property, and even their lives by signing it.
