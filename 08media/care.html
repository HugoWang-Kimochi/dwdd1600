Puppy Guide

Welcome to Puppy Care

Bringing home a puppy is exciting and fun, but it also comes with many responsibilities. Puppies need proper care, training, and attention to grow into happy and healthy dogs. This guide will help you learn the basics of puppy care.

Feeding Your Puppy

Puppies need proper nutrition to grow strong and healthy. Feeding your puppy the right food and following a consistent schedule helps support healthy development.

  • Feed 3-4 small meals per day
  • Choose high-quality puppy food
  • Always provide fresh water
  • Avoid feeding human food

Exercise and Playtime

Puppies have lots of energy and need regular exercise and playtime to stay healthy and happy. Daily activity helps them develop strong muscles and prevents boredom.

Benefits of Exercise

  • Helps puppies stay healthy
  • Builds strong muscles and bones
  • Reduces boredom and destructive behavior
  • Provides mental stimulation

Fun Activities

  • Fetch - A fun way to burn energy
  • Short walks - Helps puppies explore
  • Tug toys - Encourages interaction
  • Puzzle toys - Stimulates the brain

Grooming

Regular grooming helps keep your puppy clean, comfortable, and healthy. Grooming also allows owners to check for skin problems, parasites, or unusual changes in their puppy's body. Starting grooming early helps puppies become comfortable with being handled.

Brushing your puppy's fur helps remove dirt, loose hair, and tangles. Depending on the breed, puppies may need brushing several times a week to keep their coat healthy and smooth. Regular brushing also helps reduce shedding and keeps the coat looking clean.

Bathing your puppy occasionally helps keep their coat clean and fresh. It is important to use a gentle dog shampoo designed for puppies to avoid irritating their skin. Using human shampoo is not recommended because it can be too harsh for a puppy's skin.

Regular nail trimming and ear cleaning are also important parts of grooming. Keeping nails short helps puppies walk comfortably, while cleaning the ears helps prevent infections. Healthy grooming habits help puppies stay comfortable and prevent common health problems.

Health and Vet Care

Keeping your puppy healthy is an important part of responsible pet ownership. Regular veterinary care helps ensure your puppy grows strong and stays free from many preventable diseases. Early health care also helps detect potential problems before they become serious.

Vaccinations are an essential part of puppy health care. During the first year, puppies receive several vaccines that protect them from dangerous diseases such as parvovirus and rabies. Following your veterinarian's vaccination schedule is one of the most important steps in protecting your puppy's health.

Routine veterinary visits allow professionals to monitor your puppy's growth, weight, and overall health. These checkups also give pet owners an opportunity to ask questions about diet, behavior, and general care.

Important: If your puppy shows unusual symptoms such as loss of appetite, vomiting, extreme tiredness, or difficulty breathing, it is important to contact a veterinarian as soon as possible.
