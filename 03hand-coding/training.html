Building Good Habits

Training is not about control. It is about communication, structure, and trust. Dogs learn best when guidance is calm and consistent.

Positive Reinforcement

Positive reinforcement means rewarding good behavior instead of punishing mistakes. When your dog follows a command correctly, offer praise, a treat, or affection.

Basic Commands Every Dog Should Know

These commands create structure and improve safety:

  • Sit
  • Stay
  • Come
  • Leave it
  • Down

How to Teach “Sit” (Step-by-Step)

  1. Hold a treat slightly above your dog's nose.
  2. Slowly move the treat back over their head.
  3. As their bottom touches the floor, say “Sit.”
  4. Immediately reward and praise.
  5. Repeat for short 2 ~ 3 minute sessions.

Source from Chewy's Dog Training Guide: Chewy.com

Consistency and Patience

Short sessions are more effective than long ones. Practice daily for a few minutes. Dogs respond best when expectations are clear and repeated calmly.
